Tips
• Divide your preparation into structured phases — foundation, revision, and simulation.
• Stay consistent and disciplined; even 3 focused hours daily outweigh 10 distracted hours.
• Prioritize Bailey as your base book; add Schwartz or Sabiston selectively if time permits.
• Solve MCQs daily (50–150/day) and maintain a mistake journal for revision.
• Attempt mock exams every 5–7 days; analyze errors immediately after each test.
• Plan your last 15–20 days exclusively for revision and active recall.
• Revise using the 1–3–7–15 day cycle to strengthen long-term memory.
• Practice solving questions within 45 seconds to build exam-time efficiency.
• Don’t change your source material midway; stick to one and revise multiple times.
• Maintain a positive mindset—discipline, consistency, and smart strategy matter more than luck.
